Q: How many calories are in your non-alcoholic beers?
Our non-alcoholic beers typically contain fewer calories compared to their alcoholic counterparts, ranging from about 50 to 100 calories per serving.

Our prices vary depending on the type and quantity of non-alcoholic beer you choose. Please contact us here for the most up-to-date pricing information.
Q: Do you offer international shipping?
Yes, we offer international shipping to select countries. Shipping rates and delivery times may vary depending on your location. Please contact us here for more details.
Q: What ingredients are used in your non-alcoholic beers?
Our non-alcoholic beers are brewed using high-quality ingredients, including water, malted barley, hops, and yeast. We also use special brewing techniques to remove alcohol while preserving flavor.
Q: What are the health benefits of non-alcoholic beer?
Non-alcoholic beer offers many of the same health benefits as regular beer, including hydration, relaxation, and enjoyment of flavor, without the intoxicating effects of alcohol. It's a great option for those looking to reduce their alcohol consumption or abstain altogether.
Q: Are your non-alcoholic beers suitable for individuals with gluten intolerance?
Yes, we offer gluten-free options for individuals with gluten intolerance or celiac disease. Our gluten-free non-alcoholic beers are brewed using gluten-free grains such as sorghum, rice, or millet.
Q: Can pregnant women consume non-alcoholic beer?
While non-alcoholic beer contains minimal to no alcohol, it's best for pregnant women to consult with their healthcare provider before consuming any alcoholic or non-alcoholic beverages during pregnancy.
Q: Do your non-alcoholic beers contain artificial additives or preservatives?
No, our non-alcoholic beers are crafted using natural ingredients and do not contain artificial additives or preservatives. We believe in delivering a pure and authentic beer-drinking experience.
Q: How should I store non-alcoholic beer?
Non-alcoholic beer should be stored in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight and heat sources. It's best to store them upright to prevent any sedimentation, and once opened, they should be refrigerated and consumed within a few days for optimal freshness.
